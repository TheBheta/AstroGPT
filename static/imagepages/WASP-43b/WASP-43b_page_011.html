Exoplanet WASP-43b

jonathanxvi (62)in #science • 7 years ago

Exoplanet WASP-43b orbits its parent star. Image source: [1]

Exoplanet WASP-43b is located 260 light years from Earth, in the Constellation of the Sextant, a small constellation between the constellations of Leo and Hydra. This exoplanet has the size of Jupiter but with twice its mass and is peculiar its orbit since it is much closer to its parent star (WASP-43) than any planet in our solar system. Another curiosity is the duration of its year, one of the shorter: only 19 hours. WASP-43b

                                         

Comparative size of the WASP-43b exoplanet with the planets of our solar system. Image Credit: Science Thoughts

Wind and extreme temperatures

The exoplanet is blocked by tide, this means that like our Moon, it always shows the same face to its star, reason why it has a hemisphere permanently illuminated and another hemisphere in the dark. Observations from the Hubble Space Telescope show that this exoplanet has winds flowing from the daytime to night faces that reach a speed of over 1,200 km / h. As a curious fact, in the daytime part of the exoplanet could be melted a piece of iron since temperatures reach above 1,500 ° C. However, in its nocturnal part, the temperatures are a little smaller: about 1,000 ° C.
