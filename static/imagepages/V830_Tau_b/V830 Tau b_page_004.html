                V830 Tau b Exoplanet Facts

                V830 Tauri is located 427.27 light years or 131.00 parsecs from the Earth based on the latest parallax records from the Hipparcos satellite. It would take 427.27 years travelling at the speed of light to get there. Both the star and the planet can be located in the equatorialern hemisphere constellation of Taurus. It is not possible to see either the exoplanet or the star just looking up in the night sky as it is too dim.

                The planet has a mass that is equal to 0.770 times that of Jupiter,the largest planet in our Solar System.

                The Orbital Period, or to say it another way, V830 Tau b's year is 4.93 days or 0.01 Earth Years. By the time, the Earth has completed a full year, the planet will have completed 74.04 orbits of its star .

                Eccentricity of V830 Tau b is 0.0. It is a measure of how circular the orbital path of the exoplanet is. If the value is zero, the orbital path is a perfect circle. The max value is 1 meaning its an ellipse.

                Orbital Inclination of V830 Tau b is 55.00000. It is the angle of the path around V830 Tauri in respect to the viewing from Earth rather than from star's ecliptic.

                The Semi-Major Axis is the furthest point from its star in its orbit. The Earth travels round the Sun with a semi-major axis just over 1 A.U. ( 1.00000011). 1 A.U. is the average distance of the Earth from the Sun. V830 Tau b orbits V830 Tauri at a closer distance than Mercury does to the Sun.
